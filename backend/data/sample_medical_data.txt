DIABETES
--------
Diabetes mellitus is a group of metabolic disorders characterized by high blood sugar levels.

Type 1 Diabetes:
- Autoimmune condition where the pancreas produces little or no insulin
- Usually diagnosed in children and young adults
- Requires daily insulin injections
- Symptoms: frequent urination, excessive thirst, unintended weight loss, fatigue
- Management: insulin therapy, blood sugar monitoring, healthy diet, regular exercise

Type 2 Diabetes:
- Most common form (90-95% of cases)
- Cells don't respond normally to insulin (insulin resistance)
- Risk factors: obesity, sedentary lifestyle, family history, age over 45
- Symptoms: increased thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision, slow-healing sores
- Management: lifestyle changes, oral medications (metformin), sometimes insulin
- Complications: heart disease, nerve damage, kidney disease, eye damage

Prediabetes:
- Blood sugar is higher than normal but not high enough for type 2 diabetes diagnosis
- Fasting blood sugar: 100-125 mg/dL
- HbA1c: 5.7% - 6.4%
- Reversible with lifestyle changes

HYPERTENSION (HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE)
------------------------------------
Normal blood pressure: less than 120/80 mmHg
Elevated: 120-129 / less than 80 mmHg
Stage 1 hypertension: 130-139 / 80-89 mmHg
Stage 2 hypertension: 140 or higher / 90 or higher mmHg
Hypertensive crisis: over 180 / over 120 mmHg

Causes:
- Primary (essential) hypertension: no identifiable cause, develops gradually
- Secondary hypertension: underlying condition (kidney disease, thyroid problems, sleep apnea)

Risk Factors:
- Age (risk increases with age)
- Family history
- Being overweight or obese
- Physical inactivity
- High-sodium diet
- Smoking
- Stress
- Excessive alcohol consumption

Symptoms:
- Often called the "silent killer" — most people have no symptoms
- Severe cases: headaches, shortness of breath, nosebleeds

Treatment:
- Lifestyle changes: DASH diet, regular exercise, weight reduction, limiting alcohol
- Medications: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, calcium channel blockers, diuretics, beta-blockers
- Regular monitoring of blood pressure

Complications:
- Heart attack and heart disease
- Stroke
- Kidney damage
- Eye damage (retinopathy)
- Peripheral artery disease

COMMON COLD VS INFLUENZA
--------------------------
Common Cold:
- Caused by rhinoviruses (most common) and other viruses
- Symptoms: runny nose, sore throat, cough, congestion, mild fever (rare in adults)
- Onset: gradual
- Duration: 7-10 days
- Treatment: rest, fluids, over-the-counter symptom relief
- Serious complications: rare

Influenza (Flu):
- Caused by influenza A or B viruses
- Symptoms: fever (100-104°F), severe muscle aches, chills, headache, fatigue, dry cough
- Onset: sudden
- Duration: 1-2 weeks
- Treatment: rest, fluids, antiviral medications (oseltamivir/Tamiflu) if caught early
- Prevention: annual flu vaccine strongly recommended
- Serious complications: pneumonia, hospitalization (risk higher in elderly, young children, immunocompromised)

VACCINES AND IMMUNITY
----------------------
How vaccines work:
1. Introduce a weakened, killed, or partial form of a pathogen (or its genetic instructions)
2. Immune system recognizes it as foreign and mounts a response
3. Body produces antibodies and memory cells
4. When exposed to the real pathogen later, the immune system responds quickly
5. This prevents or reduces the severity of illness

Types of vaccines:
- Live-attenuated: weakened pathogen (MMR, chickenpox, yellow fever)
- Inactivated: killed pathogen (flu shot, polio IPV, hepatitis A)
- Subunit/protein: part of the pathogen (hepatitis B, whooping cough)
- mRNA vaccines: instructions to make a protein (COVID-19 Pfizer, Moderna)
- Viral vector: uses a modified virus to deliver instructions (COVID-19 J&J)

Herd immunity:
- When enough of a population is immune to stop disease spread
- Protects those who cannot be vaccinated
- Threshold varies by disease (measles requires ~95%, polio ~80-85%)

MENTAL HEALTH
--------------
Anxiety Disorders:
- Most common mental health condition
- Types: generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder, social anxiety, phobias
- Symptoms: excessive worry, restlessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, sleep problems
- Treatment: cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), medications (SSRIs, SNRIs, benzodiazepines)

Depression:
- Major depressive disorder characterized by persistent sadness and loss of interest
- Symptoms: persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in appetite/sleep, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, feelings of worthlessness, thoughts of death
- Treatment: psychotherapy (CBT), antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs), lifestyle changes
- Note: always seek professional help — depression is treatable

Important: If you or someone you know is in crisis or experiencing suicidal thoughts, please contact:
- National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (US): 988 or 1-800-273-8255
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
- International Association for Suicide Prevention: https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/

MEDICATION BASICS
------------------
Common medication classes:
- Antibiotics: treat bacterial infections (NOT viral infections like colds/flu)
- Analgesics: pain relievers (acetaminophen, ibuprofen, aspirin)
- Antihistamines: treat allergies (cetirizine, loratadine, diphenhydramine)
- Statins: lower cholesterol (atorvastatin, simvastatin)
- Antihypertensives: lower blood pressure (lisinopril, amlodipine, metoprolol)
- Antidiabetics: control blood sugar (metformin, insulin, glipizide)

Important notes:
- Never stop prescription medications without consulting your doctor
- Always inform your doctor about all medications and supplements you take
- Drug interactions can be dangerous
- Follow dosage instructions carefully
